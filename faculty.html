For faculty & researchers

Helping students think with AI — and measuring whether it works.

A classroom pilot on AI use and student thinking. I'm looking for a faculty partner to run, critique, and co-sign it.

VibeAI FoldSpace is a free browser extension, live in the Chrome and Edge stores, that detects low-engagement signals during AI conversations and prompts one moment of reflection. It's designed to help students stay aware of their own thinking while using AI — not to police them, and not to prove anything on its own.

The pilot exists to test whether that design actually changes behavior. Two-week, two-arm study: treatment group sees live engagement feedback, control group doesn't. Behavioral engagement is the primary outcome; cognitive-thinking measures are exploratory, using parallel forms. Minimal-risk, local-first — all data stays on-device unless a study protocol explicitly says otherwise.

Free for education, always.

Why this, why now

The research signal exists. The real-time test doesn't — yet.

A peer-reviewed study of 666 participants (Gerlich, 2025) found a strong negative correlation (r = −0.75) between AI offloading and independent critical-thinking scores. Kabashkin (2025) maps the same pattern as a four-stage progression from tool use to full cognitive externalization. And a randomized trial (Qazi et al., 2026, NEJM AI) found that even 44 physicians with 20 hours of dedicated AI-literacy training lost 14 points of diagnostic accuracy when exposed to confidently wrong AI output — despite being free to ignore it.

All of that research is correlational or measured after the fact. Nobody has tested whether real-time awareness feedback changes engagement while it's happening. That's the gap this pilot is designed to test.

The pilot design

We built the tool.
Now we want to test it — with you.

Study Design

2-week randomized pilot. Treatment group sees live VibeAI FoldSpace cognitive feedback during AI conversations. Control group uses the same AI tools with no engagement feedback shown. Pre/post behavioral mapping.

Participants

Recruit 60–70, target ~50 completing. University cohort using AI tools for coursework. Mixed AI usage frequency. Diverse academic backgrounds. Minimal risk — all data stays on device, local-first architecture.

What We Measure

Primary (behavioral): active vs. passive engagement ratio, session persistence, message depth, reflection frequency.

Exploratory (self-reported, parallel forms): pre/post survey on AI overreliance, mindfulness, and metacognition.

What We're NOT Claiming

We are not claiming this works. We are claiming it's worth testing — with enough rigor that the result, positive or negative, adds something real to the field. If real-time awareness doesn't change behavior, that finding matters just as much.
